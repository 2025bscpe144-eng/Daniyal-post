  "Starting My Computer Engineering Journey"
---

1. New Academic Environment  
Adjusting to university life was both exciting and challenging. It was a completely new journey for me.  
- New teachers and different teaching styles  
- A study system very different from college  
- More focus on self-learning and independence  

At first, I felt confused and overwhelmed. I had to learn how to manage assignments, attend lectures, and understand the university routine. Slowly, I started adapting and finding my rhythm.

2. Understanding Technical Subjects  
Computer Engineering subjects were challenging in the beginning.  
- Programming concepts were completely new  
- Logical thinking was required in every subject  
- Understanding lectures took time  

This blog marks the **beginning of my first semester journey**, where I learned to face academic challenges and stay determined.

---
  "Struggling with Programming Fundamentals (Python)"
---

1. Syntax Problems  
Programming syntax was confusing in the early days.  
- Small mistakes caused big errors in output  
- Remembering all rules was difficult  
- Debugging was frustrating  

Even a tiny missing colon or indentation error could stop the program from running, which was frustrating at first.

2. Logical Thinking  
Building logic in programming was another challenge.  
- Writing algorithms step by step  
- Understanding flow of the program  
- Solving problems systematically  

I realized that programming is not just writing code; it is about **thinking logically**.

3. Practice Requirement  
Programming requires consistent practice.  
- Exercises were time-consuming  
- Lack of regular practice slowed progress  
- Patience was required  

By the end of the semester, through consistent effort, I improved a lot and secured **B+ in Programming Fundamentals**. This achievement motivated me to keep learning.

---
  "Managing Academic Pressure and Multiple Courses"
---

1. Multiple Subjects  
Handling several courses simultaneously was challenging.  
- Assignments from different subjects  
- Continuous quizzes and deadlines  
- Preparing for exams  

Learning to prioritize and manage time became essential.

2. Stress and Anxiety  
Academic pressure sometimes led to stress.  
- Fear of failure  
- Mental pressure due to workload  
- Lack of confidence  

Over time, I developed strategies to stay calm and focused under pressure.

3. First Semester Project Exhibition  
One of the highlights of the semester was our **Machine Learning project exhibition**.  
- We trained a model to **predict price based on multiple factors**  
- Learned to clean and prepare data for the model  
- Implemented machine learning concepts in practice  

Presenting our project boosted my confidence and gave me hands-on experience in real-world applications.

---
  "Understanding and Applying Mathematics"
---

1. Complex Topics  
Mathematics in university was much more advanced.  
- Calculus and problem-solving became challenging  
- Required deep understanding and constant practice  
- Needed to connect concepts to real problems  

2. Concept Clarity  
Grasping concepts was not easy at first.  
- Weak basic knowledge sometimes created difficulty  
- Formulas could be confusing  
- Lecture pace was fast  

I had to revisit topics multiple times and practice exercises to gain clarity.

3. Application in Engineering  
Applying mathematics in engineering problems was another challenge.  
- Calculations for real-world scenarios  
- Understanding formulas in context  
- Solving exam-level problems  

Despite the initial difficulty, practicing consistently helped me build confidence in using math effectively.

---
  "First Semester Exams and Results"
---

1. Exam Preparation  
Preparing for exams was one of the most stressful experiences of the semester.  
- Large syllabus to cover  
- Limited time for revision  
- Pressure to perform well  

Organizing time and focusing on weak areas became crucial.

2. Exam Anxiety  
Fear of exams affected my confidence initially.  
- Nervousness before exams  
- Overthinking mistakes  
- Doubts about performance  

I learned to trust my preparation and stay calm during exams.

3. Time Management During Exams  
Managing time during exams was challenging.  
- Long questions required planning  
- Limited time increased pressure  
- Mistakes could occur due to rush  

4. Final Results and Reflection  
Alhamdulillah, my hard work paid off.  
- **GPA: 3.1**  
- **B+ in Programming Fundamentals**  

Reflecting on my first semester, I see how much I grew. From struggling with new subjects to completing a Machine Learning project and achieving good results, this journey taught me that **consistency, effort, and patience always lead to success**.

  "Starting Second Semester with Motivation"
---

> "Success is the sum of small efforts, repeated day in and day out." – Robert Collier

1. Reflecting on First Semester  
Alhamdulillah, I completed my first semester with a **GPA of 3.1**. This result was good, but I knew I could do better. I decided that in my **second semester**, I would aim to achieve **above 3.5 GPA**.  

- Motivation to improve performance  
- Learning from previous struggles  
- Setting higher academic goals  

With this mindset, I stepped into the new semester full of determination and focus.

2. New Challenges Ahead  
Second semester brought **new courses** and **greater responsibilities**. I knew I had to stay disciplined and consistent to reach my goals.  
- Calculus and OOP concepts  
- Digital Logic Design (DLD)  
- Communication Skills  
- Database Management with SQL  

I was ready to face challenges and work hard to improve my academic performance.

---
  "Learning Core Courses and SQL"
---

1. Calculus and OOP  
Calculus continued to be challenging, requiring careful problem-solving and consistent practice. In **OOP**, I learned concepts like classes, objects, inheritance, and polymorphism.  

- Practical programming exercises  
- Logical thinking in software design  
- Applying theory to coding projects  

2. Digital Logic Design (DLD)  
DLD was an important part of the semester. We learned about logic gates, Boolean algebra, and circuit design.  

- Hands-on exercises in circuit simulations  
- Learning to simplify expressions  
- Understanding hardware fundamentals  

3. Database Management & SQL  
Database Management was exciting.  

- Introduction to SQL and relational databases  
- Using **MySQL Workbench** for designing and querying databases  
- Practicing commands like `SELECT`, `INSERT`, `UPDATE`, and `DELETE`  
- Learning about tables, keys, and relationships  

SQL was challenging at first, but gradually I started enjoying writing queries and designing database structures.

  "First Ramadan in Hostel and Global Challenges"
---

1. Ramadan in Hostel  
This semester, I experienced **Ramadan for the first time in the hostel**, which was a new challenge.  

- Fasting while managing studies  
- Adjusting to hostel timings  
- Balancing meals, prayer, and classes  

2. Global Situation & Online Classes  
Due to the **Iran-America war**, oil prices increased rapidly, and our government decided to **shift classes online on 20th Ramadan**.  

- Studying from home  
- Adapting to online lectures  
- Enjoying Eid with family  

3. Returning to Physical Classes  
After Eid, on **1st April**, our classes shifted back to **physical mode**.  

- Experienced **3 holidays in a single week**  
- Needed to adjust back to campus routine  

This period taught me flexibility and adaptability, both academically and personally.

   "Overall Conclusion – My First Year Journey in Computer Engineering"
---

> "The journey of a thousand miles begins with a single step." – Lao Tzu

1. Reflecting on My Journey  
The first year of my **Computer Engineering journey** has been full of challenges, learning, and personal growth. From the struggles of **Programming Fundamentals** and understanding technical subjects in the first semester to tackling **Calculus, OOP, DLD, Communication Skills, and SQL** in the second semester, I have realized the importance of **hard work, persistence, and consistency**.  

2. Key Lessons Learned  
- **Persistence pays off:** Consistent effort helped me overcome difficulties in programming, math, and database management.  
- **Teamwork and leadership matter:** Leading my team in the university fiesta taught me how to manage people, handle pressure, and learn from both victories and defeats.  
- **Adaptability is essential:** Experiencing **Ramadan in the hostel**, adjusting to online classes due to global events, and sudden schedule changes taught me flexibility.  
- **Goal setting and motivation:** Setting a higher GPA goal and committing to work harder helped me stay focused and motivated throughout the second semester.  

3. Looking Ahead  
This first year strengthened my **academic skills, problem-solving abilities, and personal growth**. I am now more confident, disciplined, and ready to face new challenges in the upcoming semesters. My aim is to **continue improving, maintain focus, and achieve excellence** in both studies and personal development.  

The first year has been just the beginning. With the lessons learned and experiences gained, I am excited for the rest of my **Computer Engineering journey** and the opportunities that lie ahead.
